May 18 - May 24 Learning

Last week I did pretty well with what I learned, but this week I have a serious amount of ambition. I need a job. Soon. And as of right now I have had very little success finding employers who will take my skills. To change that, I am doing an additional course this week to increase my marketability now that I have an idea of some of the requirements of a security expert. I have a few other tasks planned of course, but this week I am focusing on completing this course as quickly as possible.

My intended focuses:

  • Complete OverTheWire Bandit CTF.
  • Update Passwords
  • Start Unreal Engine Learning
  • Finish Up Final Work for the Cyber Range

Repeating Activities include:

  • Continue Web Developer Bootcamp
  • Read and complete PFsense Book/Course
  • HTB (Retired) Walkthroughs
  • Apply for Jobs

Additional Non-Focused Activities:

  • Improve OpenVPN Autodeployment script (v1.2)
  • Hackintosh VM
  • Security Onion Research
  • Pivot Tables Research

Now I’ll admit; this list is a bit intense especially with the PFsense course being the heavy hitting focus of this week. It will take up a lot of my time, and my other intended focuses will in fact compete for that valuable time. My plan is to execute most of the intended focuses on the weekend while keeping the course focuses for this week. If I get discouraged or distracted with some particular task, I have non-focused activities that I can work with to give myself a break.

I most likely will not complete everything on this list, so my hope is to complete all of my intended focuses and repeating activities. If I can finish one or two of my non-focused activities I’ll be satisfied and deem the sprint a success. I’ll be back at the end of the week to report on how I did, but in the meantime expect a report on my previous week’s completion of the Intro to Windows Exploit Development Course. Don’t forget to checkout my Trello Board to see what I am working on day-to-day.
