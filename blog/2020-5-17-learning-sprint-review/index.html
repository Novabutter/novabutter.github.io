May 13th- May 17th Learning Review

This past week I did pretty well with my productivity. I had just finished finals and started my sprint really on Wednesday, so I had about 5 days. For that time I was decently productive and effectively accomplished many of my tasks. My focus was pretty unhindered minus me falling slightly ill tonight toward the end and I am proud of what I accomplished. Here were my focuses and what I got done.

My intended focuses:

  • Complete OverTheWire Bandit CTF.
  • Attempt to finish SMTP & IMAP Lab
  • NTP & Nextcloud Servers Lab
  • Finish Up Final Work for the Cyber Range

Repeating Activities include:

  • Continue Web Developer Bootcamp
  • Continue Introduction to Windows Exploit Development Course (courtesy of Connor McGarr)
  • Apply for Jobs

Additional Non-Focused Activities:

  • Update Passwords
  • Finish OpenVPN Autodeployment script (v1.0)
  • Update github blog appearance
  • HTB (Retired) Walkthroughs

Review

Okay okay I know. It may not look like a lot, and it honestly appears that I botched my intended focuses entirely. In a list or prioritizing my course learning over nearly most other things I did well. My 3 of my intended focuses I had slated to accomplish tonight, which may have been a bit eager but regardless I fell ill and wasn’t able to work on them for a large amount of precious hours.

What I did gladly finish was the Windows Exploit Development course. That was a ton of fun and I learned quite a bit; your should expect a blog post by the end of the week on what I learned. I worked on Web Development as well and applied for jobs despite the slow market. In terms of my non-foused activities, I did touch everything on the list. I finished my OpenVPN script (partially as it still has quite a bit of bugs but it does technically work as of the moment), I did complete my random password generation script (also partially) that is associated with my attempting to change my passwords. The changing of the passwords I was not able to get to and thus will roll-over into the coming week. I watched a few HackTheBox walkthroughs and of course as you can see, I updated my blog appearance which in itself took nearly a day to learn how to deploy hugo.

When it comes to my intended focuses not being completed, I am honestly okay with this. My actual “focus” was on working through my courses of which I have been efficiently. While I still have a small bit of work to finish up for the Cyber Range and I would like to complete the OverTheWire Bandit CTF, the other two I have no intended focus on. These are more system administration tasks to expand the capabilities of my virtual laboratory, and while fine I really had them on the list as more of a focus to attempt to accomplish because they’ve been on my list so long anyway. As I am moving away from system administration and more into security, I’ve decided to move these items far into the future as they are not a priority with the CTF and work being rolled into the upcoming week. Only if I have time will I continue to develop my other scripts as well.

Takeaway

Overall I did pretty well even if it doesn’t appear so. I’m proud of what I have done so far and I was decently efficient. What I could do to improve is not substantial; just watch a bit less Netflix and try not to get sick. Again, this upcoming week will be heavily course-focused so my tasks will be less than usual.

Thanks for listening, and see you tomorrow for an deceleration.
