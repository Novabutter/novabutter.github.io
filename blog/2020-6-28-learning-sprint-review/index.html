June 22 - June 28 Learning Review

I truly feel like a child sometimes shooting blindly at cool things I observe or look at. Then I get sidetracked but feel like I am probably doing a task worthwhile when I am in reality completely missing the target. When will I learn? Plenty of time for opportunity all squandered by who knows what? Definitely could have done more this week even though I had little distractions. I only have a few guesses as to what happened. Here is what I did manage to accomplish:

My intended focuses:

  • Start Unreal Engine Learning
  • Windows Exploit Development Course Review Post

Repeating Activities include:

  • Read and complete PFsense Book/Course
  • Study for PCNSA Exam
  • Start Active Directory Learning
  • HTB (Retired) Walkthroughs
  • Apply for Jobs

Additional Non-Focused Activities:

  • Improve OpenVPN Autodeployment script (v1.2)
  • Security Onion Research

Review

Worst week yet? Probably. I had a ton of opportunity to get many things done, I just didn’t end up having a chance to do them. Which is odd because—nevermind you don’t care about excuses. And neither do I. What I did get done was my Windows Exploit Development Post. That took many hours more than I had expected (I thought maybe an hour or two tops but it ended up being like 10 or so). As far as the rest of the items go, I did do some light studying for the PCNSA, but not nearly enough. As for the rest I was simply occupied either by the post or by me going out of town for the week.

Again, rollover is bound to happen, but truly what went wrong this week? Maybe I didn’t have a plan day-to-day and didn’t prioritize items enough but I do assure you I was busy nearly all week. So what needs to change?

Takeaway

I did not apply the new philosophy that I had mentioned, which I believe to be a large reason that I did not succeed in accomplishing all of these tasks. To add to this, I had little daily plan on what items I would do. I still have been applying the previous techniques of only taking what I think I can actually accomplish in a day as well as leaving time for error or the like. Regardless, I’ll be moving on this upcoming week by setting a daily plan, going to bed at a decent hour, and following my schedule religiously regardless of exceptions or too much time investment. Onward to less side-tracking!
